Current Exhibition

Surrealism & Perspective

Exploring the Boundaries of Reality

Location Main Gallery
Dates January 1 - March 31, 2026
Admission Free with Museum Entry

About the Exhibition

This exhibition explores the boundaries of reality through three interconnected works that challenge conventional perception and invite viewers into a world of imaginative possibility. Drawing from the rich traditions of surrealism, the exhibition examines how artistic vision transforms our understanding of space, time, and consciousness.

Each artwork in this exhibition represents a different approach to surrealist expression, from the mathematical precision inspired by M.C. Escher to the dreamlike qualities reminiscent of Salvador Dal√≠. The works collectively investigate how perception can be manipulated, reality can be questioned, and new perspectives can emerge through artistic intervention.

Visitors are invited to engage with the works not just as observers, but as participants in a dialogue about the nature of reality. The exhibition challenges viewers to question what they see, how they see it, and why certain interpretations emerge over others.

Featured Artworks

Memory Orb I

The first in a series of three interconnected works exploring memory, perspective, and the distortion of reality through spherical lenses.

Memory Orb II

Continuing the exploration of memory and perception, this work examines how personal history can be refracted and reinterpreted.

Memory Orb III

The final piece in the trilogy, completing the examination of how memory shapes our understanding of space and time.
